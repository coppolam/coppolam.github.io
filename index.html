Designing robotic swarms

Imagine large swarms of cheap robots that can coordinately lift loads together, explore large areas, and build structures.

Two challenges remain to be solved:
1. the top-down automatic development of local rules from a global goal.
2. the bottom-up verification of whether the local rules will lead to the desired global goal.

In this project, I am trying to find new algorithms to solve these open challenges.

On-board relative localization for drones

The first step to collaboration is knowing where your neighbors are. This is still an ongoing challenge in robotics.

In this project, we have developed on-board solutions that can fit on even the smallest drones, simply based on communication via antennas. This solution is light-weight and energy efficient. We demonstrated accurate results in indoor scenarios, where GPS and other such infrastructure is not available.

Swarmulator

Swarmulator is a lightweight C++ simulator for simulating swarms. Swarmulator offers a simple platform to prototype swarm behaviors, used in multiple publications (see git branches).
